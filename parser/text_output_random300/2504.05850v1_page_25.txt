It is worth noting that periods of graphs with fermion edges are more prone to weight mixing than
periods of graphs in pure ϕ4 theory, see, e.g., the last graph in Figure 11.
In future work, one can use identities to relate unknown Feynman periods to (sums of) known Feynman
periods. Alternatively (ideally: additionally) one can calculate kernel graphical functions using identities
or by mapping them to scalar graphical functions. These can either be calculated by the existing version
of HyperlogProcedures [23] or one can try to solve them with IBP methods. A combined method that
acts on periods and graphical functions has been used successfully in six-dimensional ϕ3 theory [3].
We plan to compute the five loop contribution to the beta functions of Yukawa-ϕ4 theory in the near
future.
